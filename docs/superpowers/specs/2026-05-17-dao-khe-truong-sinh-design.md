# Design Spec: Đạo Khế Trường Sinh

## Purpose

Design a new long-form Vietnamese xianxia novel, separate from `Mưa Trên Bến Không Đèn`.

The story should follow orthodox xianxia progression while keeping a light dark tone: the cultivation world is competitive, compromised, and often unfair, but not nihilistic. The protagonist is practical and morally bounded rather than purely heroic or purely ruthless.

## Working Title

`Đạo Khế Trường Sinh`

The title carries the central premise:

- `Đạo`: the path of cultivation.
- `Khế`: contract, debt, pledge, and binding price.
- `Trường Sinh`: the great promise of cultivation, and the greatest debt.

## Core Promise

Cultivation is not a gift. It is a debt.

Every immortal opportunity is a contract. Spiritual energy is borrowed capital. Breakthroughs are renewed debts. Heart demons are creditors. Heavenly tribulation is collection day. The higher a cultivator climbs, the less free they may become, because their debts bind more lives, causes, and worlds.

The novel follows Lâm Thất, an ordinary and practical boy from a mortal village, as he enters a declining righteous sect to save his family from a sect contract and search for his missing older brother. His only unusual advantage is the `Sổ Nợ Vô Danh`, a nameless black ledger that can reveal fragments of hidden debts.

## Genre And Tone

- Primary genre: orthodox xianxia.
- Secondary texture: sect politics, mystery, debt-contract intrigue, slow-burn romance.
- Tone: light dark, between upright sect adventure and harsher survival cultivation.
- Violence and exploitation exist, but the story should preserve room for loyalty, restraint, gratitude, and difficult mercy.

Avoid:

- Easy chosen-one destiny.
- Free power-ups without price.
- Villains who are evil only because the plot needs them to be.
- Harem progression.
- Long lecture-style exposition about cultivation rules.

Prefer:

- Practical decisions under pressure.
- Rules revealed through contracts, missions, punishments, and loopholes.
- Moral costs attached to progress.
- Opponents with defensible logic and compromised choices.
- Wins that solve one debt while creating another.

## Protagonist

### Lâm Thất

Lâm Thất is not a genius, not a secretly supreme bloodline heir, and not a comic "trash root" waiting for one miraculous treasure. He is ordinary, observant, patient, and deeply practical.

Initial motivations:

- Enter Linh Khê Tông to keep his family from being crushed by the sect's recruitment debt.
- Find the truth about his older brother, Lâm Trường An, who was recruited years earlier and never truly returned.

Moral boundaries:

- He can deceive, endure humiliation, set traps, and kill when necessary.
- He does not betray someone who truly saved him.
- He does not kill uselessly just to prove resolve.
- He does not use "the great Dao" as a blanket excuse for every cruelty.

Narrative function:

Lâm Thất wins by reading systems, people, debts, and omissions. He should not win repeatedly through luck. When he uses the Sổ Nợ Vô Danh, it gives information, not answers.

## Core Artifact

### Sổ Nợ Vô Danh

A black ledger without a title, first found in the old belongings of Lâm Trường An.

Capabilities:

- Reveals partial debts attached to people, contracts, places, and techniques.
- Can show debts of spiritual energy, life, oath, sect, technique, blood, karma, or fate.
- Does not directly increase cultivation.
- Does not explain the full truth.
- Can be misread if Lâm Thất lacks context.

Cost:

- Each meaningful use writes an unclear debt line against Lâm Thất.
- The ledger may reveal only what is owed, not who benefits.
- Later arcs reveal the ledger is tied to an ancient heavenly contract.

First activation:

When Lục Hoài Chân reads the recruitment contract in Lâm Thất's village, the ledger displays:

```text
Khế nhập đạo đã lập.
Người vay: Lâm Thất.
Nợ gốc: một đời phàm thân.
Lãi: chưa định.
```

## Cultivation System

Use a familiar xianxia realm ladder, but reinterpret each level as a deeper debt relationship with heaven and earth.

```text
Luyện Khí -> Trúc Cơ -> Kim Đan -> Nguyên Anh -> Hóa Thần -> later realms
```

Realm logic:

- `Luyện Khí`: borrowing spiritual breath into the body.
- `Trúc Cơ`: using the Dao foundation as collateral.
- `Kim Đan`: forming a major contract with heaven and earth; "tribulation interest" begins.
- `Nguyên Anh`: splitting part of the self into an internal creditor and guarantor.
- `Hóa Thần`: debt stops being purely personal and begins binding sect, bloodline, karma, and inherited cause.

Rules:

- Stronger techniques can cultivate faster but carry heavier interest.
- Sect resources always come with explicit or hidden repayment.
- Cheap cultivation methods often cost time, body, status, or future ceiling.
- Forbidden methods shift debt onto others; this is effective but corrupting.
- Heavenly tribulation is not random punishment. It is the settlement of accumulated imbalance.

## Main Cast

### Lâm Trường An

Lâm Thất's older brother.

Public story:

- Recruited into Linh Khê Tông years before.
- Said to be in secluded cultivation.
- Used as proof that the Lâm family received immortal favor.

Hidden truth:

- Still alive.
- Imprisoned in `Khê Tâm Động`.
- Used as a "mạch nhân", a human anchor who pays with Dao foundation and lifespan to stabilize the failing Linh Khê spiritual vein.
- Left behind a small clue connected to the Sổ Nợ Vô Danh.

### Tạ Thanh Nghi

Slow-burn female lead.

Role:

- Daughter of the contract-administration deacon.
- Assigned to supervise new disciples.
- Initially believes sect contracts preserve fairness: whoever receives resources must repay.
- Begins as someone Lâm Thất distrusts because she represents the system binding his family.
- Distrusts Lâm Thất because he reacts too calmly to debt and pressure.

Arc:

- Learns that her father and the sect have concealed a forbidden class of contract.
- Moves from enforcing law to questioning who the law protects.
- Romance with Lâm Thất is slow, rare, and earned through choices rather than rescue scenes.

Rules:

- She has her own goals and loyalties.
- She should not exist to soften Lâm Thất or reward him.
- Her trust should cost her status, family certainty, and eventually safety.

### Lục Hoài Chân

Primary antagonist of the first arc.

Role:

- Elder responsible for spiritual-root recruitment.
- Recruited Lâm Trường An years ago.
- Returns to recruit Lâm Thất.

Character logic:

- Gentle, orderly, persuasive, and publicly righteous.
- Does not enjoy cruelty.
- Believes a declining righteous sect may sacrifice a few low-status disciples to protect many villages.
- Converts exploitation into doctrine.

Function:

He is the first face of the story's central danger: people who can turn debt into morality.

### Linh Khê Tông

The opening sect.

Past:

- Once a genuinely righteous sect.
- Protected mortal settlements from beasts, rogue cultivators, drought, and spiritual disasters.
- Opened spiritual fields and gave some mortals real upward paths.

Present:

- Its spiritual vein is drying.
- Its fields produce less.
- Its best disciples leave for greater sects.
- Administrators tighten contracts to keep the sect alive.

Conflict:

- Some elders still want to preserve the old righteousness.
- Some believe sacrificing weaker disciples is necessary.
- Some profit from the contract system and hide behind survival rhetoric.

## Structure

Target scale: 200-300 chapters.

Recommended total: about 260-300 chapters across 3 volumes and 15 arcs. If the writing needs a tighter 240-chapter version, the compression should happen inside arcs 9-15 rather than by cutting the Linh Khê Tông foundation arcs.

### Volume 1: Nợ Nhập Đạo

This volume covers the mortal village, outer sect survival, discovery of contract exploitation, the truth of Lâm Trường An, and Lâm Thất's break from Linh Khê Tông.

#### Arc 1: Ba Tháng Ngoại Môn

Target length: 18 chapters.

Lâm Thất is recruited from his mortal village into Linh Khê Tông. He discovers that recruitment is framed as immortal favor but written like debt bondage. He finds the Sổ Nợ Vô Danh in Lâm Trường An's old belongings and survives his first three months as an outer disciple.

Arc end:

- Lâm Thất avoids the first serious contract trap.
- He delays or partially repays the family's first debt.
- He gains a clue that Lâm Trường An is not in normal seclusion.

#### Arc 2: Khế Công Pháp

Target length: 20-24 chapters.

Lâm Thất must choose a cultivation method. Higher-ranked techniques cultivate faster but carry heavier hidden debt. He chooses a low-status method with tolerable interest and discovers disciples whose Dao foundations were damaged by over-signing.

Arc end:

- Lâm Thất enters stable Luyện Khí.
- Tạ Thanh Nghi begins doubting whether all contracts are fair.
- The ledger reveals that some techniques owe debt to a location under the sect.

#### Arc 3: Khê Tâm Động

Target length: 24-28 chapters.

Lâm Thất follows clues toward the sealed heart of the spiritual vein. The truth emerges: selected disciples have been used as human anchors to keep Linh Khê alive.

Arc end:

- Lâm Thất confirms Lâm Trường An is alive.
- He cannot rescue him yet.
- Lục Hoài Chân becomes personally aware that Lâm Thất is dangerous.

#### Arc 4: Nợ Máu Ngoại Môn

Target length: 20-24 chapters.

A death in the outer sect is pinned on Lâm Thất. To survive, he investigates oath debts, life debts, and forged mission contracts. This is the first strong mystery-cultivation arc.

Arc end:

- Lâm Thất clears himself without revealing the ledger.
- He exposes one middle-level exploiter.
- The deeper system remains intact.

#### Arc 5: Nội Môn Nhập Cục

Target length: 24-28 chapters.

Lâm Thất is pulled into inner sect politics not because he is honored, but because every faction wants to use his knowledge or silence him. Tạ Thanh Nghi's position becomes unstable.

Arc end:

- Lâm Thất reaches the edge of Trúc Cơ preparation.
- He learns the sect's decline is worse than advertised.
- Tạ Thanh Nghi discovers a forbidden contract category in her father's records.

#### Arc 6: Bí Cảnh Tàn Khế

Target length: 28-32 chapters.

Linh Khê disciples enter an old ruin to seek a way to save the spiritual vein. The ruin belonged to a destroyed sect that tried to erase cultivation debt entirely.

Arc end:

- Lâm Thất obtains a fragment explaining the ledger's ancient origin.
- A rescue method for Lâm Trường An becomes possible but expensive.
- Someone above Lục Hoài Chân is revealed to be using Linh Khê as collateral.

#### Arc 7: Tông Môn Sụp Một Nửa

Target length: 24-30 chapters.

Forbidden contracts leak. Some elders attempt reform, others try suppression, and opportunists use chaos to seize resources. Lục Hoài Chân is forced to choose between doctrine and direct cruelty.

Arc end:

- Linh Khê Tông fractures.
- Lâm Thất pays a serious personal cost to keep Tạ Thanh Nghi and Lâm Trường An's rescue path alive.
- The ledger writes a debt line that Lâm Thất cannot understand.

#### Arc 8: Rời Núi Linh Khê

Target length: 20-24 chapters.

Lâm Thất frees or partly frees Lâm Trường An, but cannot save everyone in Khê Tâm Động. He leaves Linh Khê branded as a traitor or contract-breaker.

Arc end:

- Lâm Thất exits the beginner sect.
- Lâm Trường An survives but is damaged and no longer simply the ideal older brother.
- Tạ Thanh Nghi must decide whether to remain and repair the sect or leave with Lâm Thất.

### Volume 2: Thành Vạn Khế

This volume expands the world. Linh Khê was not an aberration; it was a small version of the entire cultivation economy.

#### Arc 9: Thành Vạn Khế

Target length: 30-36 chapters.

Lâm Thất enters a cultivation city where almost every transaction, duel, apprenticeship, auction, pill, and protection arrangement is contract-bound. He learns to survive among loose cultivators, merchants, alchemists, formation masters, and sect agents.

Arc end:

- Lâm Thất establishes a fragile foothold.
- He learns that contract law between cultivators is older than most sects.
- The ledger reacts to the city's central contract monument.

#### Arc 10: Chủ Nợ Trời Đất

Target length: 28-34 chapters.

The Sổ Nợ Vô Danh is connected to an ancient heavenly contract. Lâm Thất discovers that many sects unknowingly cultivate under inherited debt terms.

Arc end:

- This is the midpoint revelation, not the finale.
- Lâm Thất realizes the heavenly debt system can be negotiated, distorted, or weaponized.
- A larger antagonist group notices the ledger.

#### Arc 11: Minh Ước Đại Tông

Target length: 24-30 chapters.

Great sects gather under the pretext of settling disputes and regulating rogue contracts. In reality, they are dividing access to spiritual veins and debt-bearing populations.

Arc end:

- Lâm Thất learns how righteous orders maintain clean public hands.
- Tạ Thanh Nghi's contract knowledge becomes valuable and dangerous.
- Lâm Thất makes an enemy among true major-sect heirs.

#### Arc 12: Đan Nợ Và Kiếp Lãi

Target length: 24-30 chapters.

To advance, Lâm Thất needs pills and a foundation route, but every option carries hidden cost. The arc focuses on body debt, pill debt, lifespan interest, and the first serious breakthrough crisis.

Arc end:

- Lâm Thất completes a major realm transition or secures the foundation for one.
- The ledger demands a named repayment for the first time.
- Lâm Thất refuses an easy path that would shift his debt onto innocents.

### Volume 3: Trường Sinh Đòi Nợ

This volume resolves the origin of the ledger, the heavenly contract system, and Lâm Thất's final position on what cultivation should cost.

#### Arc 13: Cựu Chủ Sổ Nợ

Target length: 24-30 chapters.

The previous holder or maker of the ledger enters the story through remnants, disciples, or a surviving will. Lâm Thất learns that every attempt to escape debt created a worse debtor.

Arc end:

- The ledger's origin is mostly revealed.
- Lâm Thất understands why it chose someone ordinary rather than a genius.
- Lâm Trường An's damaged state becomes relevant to unlocking a humane solution.

#### Arc 14: Thiên Khế Sắp Đến Hạn

Target length: 28-36 chapters.

A great repayment cycle approaches. Sects prepare by transferring debt onto weaker cultivators, mortal lands, and failed disciples. Lâm Thất and allies must decide whether to expose the truth and risk chaos.

Arc end:

- The heavenly contract begins collection.
- Major powers reveal their true methods.
- Lâm Thất stops trying to simply save his own circle and moves toward changing the rule itself.

#### Arc 15: Đạo Khế Trường Sinh

Target length: 32-45 chapters.

Final arc. Lâm Thất does not erase the price of cultivation. Instead, he attempts to rewrite the rule of debt: whoever borrows must repay; the strong cannot use the weak as collateral without consent; sects cannot hide forbidden debts under righteous doctrine.

Final shape:

- Victory is meaningful but not absolute.
- Cultivation remains costly.
- Trường sinh is not made free; it is made accountable.
- Lâm Thất's final success should preserve his core principle: debt may be inevitable, but stolen debt is evil.

## Arc 1 Chapter Skeleton

Use this as the immediate writing target after approval and planning.

1. The old chest of Lâm Trường An is opened before recruitment.
2. Lục Hoài Chân arrives and reads the recruitment contract.
3. The Sổ Nợ Vô Danh awakens.
4. Lâm Thất signs to protect his family from immediate debt collection.
5. The recruited disciples leave the village.
6. First arrival at Linh Khê Tông; immortal scenery and contract registration.
7. Outer sect housing debt and food debt are introduced.
8. Tạ Thanh Nghi audits the new disciples.
9. Lâm Thất notices a loophole in a low-grade task contract.
10. A stronger new disciple over-signs for a better technique.
11. First mission outside the outer compound.
12. Lâm Thất uses the ledger and receives his first unclear personal debt line.
13. Lục Hoài Chân asks about Lâm Trường An.
14. Tạ Thanh Nghi catches Lâm Thất hiding contract details.
15. The first contract trap closes around several new disciples.
16. Lâm Thất saves himself and one other person, but cannot save everyone.
17. He sends the first partial repayment or delay notice home.
18. The ledger reveals that Lâm Trường An's debt is still active under Linh Khê.

## Writing Rules

- Open each chapter with concrete pressure: a contract deadline, missing person, failed repayment, mission danger, body damage, accusation, or discovery.
- End each chapter by changing the meaning of an earlier detail.
- Avoid exposition dumps; explain rules by showing people paying, exploiting, breaking, or misunderstanding them.
- Give every major faction a benefit, fear, and secret.
- Give every cultivation gain a visible cost.
- Make Lâm Thất's victories intellectual and moral before they are martial.
- Keep the romance slow and sparse.
- Do not make Tạ Thanh Nghi always agree with Lâm Thất.
- Do not make Lục Hoài Chân foolish or cartoonish.
- The ledger should complicate decisions as often as it solves them.

## Success Criteria

The design succeeds if it supports a long webnovel with:

- Clear xianxia progression.
- A distinctive debt-contract cultivation premise.
- A practical but bounded protagonist.
- A slow and consequential romance.
- A complete 3-volume structure with a real ending.
- Immediate writing guidance for Arc 1.
